# RoleForge Commercial Licensing

> **TL;DR:** RoleForge Core is free for personal, education, research, and internal business use. Startups under $1M ARR are also free. Want to sell it as a service, embed it commercially, or need custom roles? That's what we charge for.

---

## The Philosophy

We built RoleForge to solve a real problem: AI agent teams need coherent personas, not fragile prompt engineering.

The **core engine is free** — anyone should be able to build with it. But extracting commercial value from our work without giving back is parasitic.

The Elastic License 2.0 handles the legal guardrails. This document handles the commercial relationship.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I just use the roles internally at my company?**  
A: Free. No license needed. Internal use is explicitly allowed under Elastic License 2.0.

**Q: Can I build my own roles using the free engine?**  
A: Absolutely. The free Starter Pack + engine lets you build unlimited custom roles.

**Q: What if I build a product that uses RoleForge roles but my customers never see the YAMLs?**  
A: Still requires an Enterprise License if you're selling a commercial product derived from RoleForge. The value is derived from our work.

**Q: Can I fork the repo and remove the license?**  
A: Legally, no. The Elastic License explicitly prohibits this. Practically, we trust the community and don't hunt people down. We just ask you to do the right thing.

**Q: What if my startup is pre-revenue?**  
A: Free until you hit $1M ARR. At that point, an Enterprise License applies.

**Q: Do I get the source code if I pay?**  
A: You already have it. The entire repo is public. You're paying for convenience, support, and legal rights.

**Q: Why not just sell individual roles?**  
A: Market research shows individual prompts sell for $3-$15 and attract hobbyists. Bundles at $49-$199 attract professionals with real budgets. See [research_output/market_research_report.md](research_output/market_research_report.md) for full analysis.
